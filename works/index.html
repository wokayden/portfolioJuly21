My Process

1) USER RESEARCH

First thing’s first - we need to figure out what your user base wants. I embark on the process by hosting 5 interviews with relevant questions and activities.

2) COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

I like to move things along by looking at what the competition is doing right and wrong. Believe it or not, some great insights can come from this.

3) HEURISTIC EVALUATION

Next, I take a look at the existing site and any issues that may be arising from the UI, information architecture, tone, branding, and copy.

4) VISUAL DESIGN

Here’s where I think harder about the kind of imagery we should be involving in relation to the brand and goals. Mood boards & style guides go a long way.

5) USER FlLOWS AND SITEMAP

I implement card sorting from my previous learnings to create an improved information architecture. We need to keep things simple for users.

6) LO-FI PROTO & USER TESTING

Now that structure is in place, it’s time to craft a low-fidelity prototype and test it on potential users to gain insights on usability and what is most intuitive.

7) TAKEAWAYS & ITERATION

I use what I learned from user tests to create new ideas and strategies for your final product. What will put what you provide above the rest?

8) HIGH-FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

In the final stretch, I use all the insights gained during the process - your insights, the visual design, improvements to accessibility - to create a delightful and engaging product.
